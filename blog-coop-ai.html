What is Cooperative Economics?

Cooperative economics is an alternative to traditional capitalism, focusing on shared ownership, mutual benefit, and equitable resource distribution. Unlike hierarchical corporate structures where a few control wealth and decision-making, cooperatives distribute profits and power among their members.

How Cooperative Models Work

  • Worker Cooperatives: Employees own and manage the company, sharing profits and governance.
  • Consumer Cooperatives: Customers collectively own businesses (e.g., credit unions, food co-ops).
  • Platform Cooperatives: Digital platforms where users share control and revenue.
  • Investment Cooperatives: Members pool resources to fund projects, keeping profits within the community.

Why It Matters

In an era of growing wealth disparity, cooperative economics offers a **more sustainable, resilient, and fair** economic model. Studies show that cooperatives tend to have higher job stability, stronger community reinvestment, and better long-term viability compared to traditional businesses.
